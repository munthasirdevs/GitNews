Our Story

Founded in 1995 by veteran journalists Robert Johnson and Sarah Williams, Global News Network began as a small news agency with a big vision: to create a truly global news organization free from political and commercial influence.

What started as a team of 25 journalists in London has grown into one of the world's most respected news organizations, with bureaus in every major region and a network of correspondents spanning six continents.

Through wars, elections, economic crises, and pandemics, GNN has been there—providing context, challenging narratives, and giving voice to the voiceless. Our commitment to investigative journalism has exposed corruption, championed human rights, and held power to account.

Robert Johnson

Co-Founder & Editor-in-Chief Emeritus

Our Mission & Values

We believe journalism is a public service. Our mission is to inform, educate, and empower citizens around the world with accurate, independent, and impactful news reporting.

Accuracy First

Every fact is verified, every source is checked, and every story is reviewed by multiple editors before publication.

Independence

We are free from political and commercial influence, funded by our readers and viewers through subscriptions.

Diversity & Inclusion

Our newsroom reflects the world we cover, with journalists from diverse backgrounds and perspectives.

Accountability

We hold power to account—whether corporate, political, or institutional—and we correct errors transparently.

Editorial Standards

Our commitment to journalistic integrity is outlined in our Editorial Standards, which every GNN journalist must follow.

Fact-Based Reporting

All information must be verified through multiple independent sources.

Source Protection

We protect confidential sources and never reveal their identities.

Conflict Disclosure

All potential conflicts of interest must be disclosed to readers.

Correction Policy

Errors are corrected promptly and transparently with clear explanations.

Code of Ethics

Our journalists pledge to uphold these principles:

  • Seek truth and report it
  • Minimize harm
  • Act independently
  • Be accountable and transparent
  • Respect diverse viewpoints
  • Protect the vulnerable
  • Challenge discrimination
  • Maintain privacy when appropriate
